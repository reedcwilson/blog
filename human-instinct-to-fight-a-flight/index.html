human instinct to fight a flight

04 February 2014

Instead of fighting or ‘flighting’, people have started to fight against flight. Drones have become an increasing realization. The closer we come to having automated and convenient flight capabilities the more people seem to reject the technology. Whether it is true or not, some companies claim that they have the technology to have drones fly to exact geographic coordinates and drive cars without accidents. Many critics say that automated flight produces dangers and could cause the company financial loss if a malicious person shot down a drone for the goods the drones deliver. Such a complaint seems like a desperate flailing attempt to fight against technological advance. Sure, there are dangers and downsides to having automated flight but the technology is swiftly surpassing human capabilities, and we may see the day when computers simply do a better job than we do.

Reed Wilson
